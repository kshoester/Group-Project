Ease of Active Transit Index
Population Density

Population density is a reliable metric for estimating the overall walkability of a neighborhood. Typically, denser areas have other resources, notably schools, within a shorter walking distance. This face is because a service, say a school, needs a certain number of people to sustain it, so the more dense that an area is the less area that a service needs to take up to sustain itself. The higher the density, the more accessible the neighborhood.

Collisions Density

A key measure of safety for active transit, these are collisions between motor vehicles and people or cyclists (normalized by neighborhood area). The goal is to highlight areas that may have good factors of active transit, but could be more dangerous and vice versa. The lower the density, the more accessible the neighborhood.

Bike Theft Rate

Specifically relating to cycling as active transit, this data from 2019 shows how many bikes were stolen by neighborhood normalized by population. The lower the rate, the more accessible the neighborhood.

Maximum Speed Limit

The highest speed limit (excluding highways) by neighborhood. The faster that a car is moving, the higher the likelyhood is of a crash leading to an injury or death. The lower the speed limit, the more accessible the neighborhood.

Sidewalk Density

The total length of sidewalk, pedestrian pathways, and cycling paths divided by the area of the neighborhood. This density is a rough estimate of how much of the neighborhood's transportation network is dedicated to active transportation as oppposed to other means (say streetcar track or road). The higher the density, the more accessible the neighborhood.
